MDP vs FDFO: How Updates Are Computed

The MDP approach reinforces random noise at every step; FDFO extracts a clean signal from paired trajectories

MDP Approach (Flow-GRPO)

Each denoising step is a separate "action" with injected noise. After reward, all perturbations are reinforced equally.

FDFO Approach (Ours)

Two trajectories from the same noise. The reward difference gives a clean update direction.

Left: MDP injects noise at each step, then reinforces all perturbations equally — most signal is noise. Right: FDFO compares paired samples and extracts a clean gradient from their reward difference.
